Pre-design · positioning

/twt-positioning

Define your positioning — audience, value props, priorities — and validate it in one pass.

Version 1.1.4 Arguments: yes

Purpose

Sharpens how your brand shows up — who it's for, the value it offers, and what to lead with — then reviews it for clarity in one pass. It turns a fuzzy idea into a focused message the whole site can rally around.

Skills it runs, in order

This skill is an orchestrator — it dispatches the skills below (via the Agent tool) rather than doing their work itself. Here's each one, in the order it runs, and why.

  1. twt-positioning-defineBuilds or refines the positioning statement with you.
  2. twt-positioning-validateChecks it for clarity and alignment with the brand.

Parameters it can expect

Optional; runs define then the bounded validate loop

What it reads

  • .twt-artifacts/pre-design/positioning/positioning.mdYour positioning: who the site is for, the value it offers, and what to promote first.
  • .twt-artifacts/pre-design/positioning/validation-report.mdA read-only critique of this artifact, listing blockers, warnings, and suggestions.

Result it provides

No files of its own — it orchestrates other skills or reports back to you.

Boundaries

  • Dispatches sub-skills (rule 5); no inline logic
  • No unbounded loop, no auto-downgrade of severity

Success criteria

  • Produces/refines positioning.md + current validation-report.md
  • Honors the §9 single-pass policy: one define + one validate (folded into define under orchestration), at most one BLOCKER-driven re-run, no score-chasing loop; reports final Band + Health and surfaces open decisions per §13 (or bubbles them up in collect mode)
  • On exit, states whether BLOCKERs remain
